## Archived DOCX: Sales script for API differentiators and value.docx

- Talking points on API differentiators and value
- @Sales
- —
- There is a distinct difference between the standalone pricing engine (Polly PPE) and the recently introduced API offering. The purpose of this guide is to highlight what our API offerings are today and how to communicate their difference in comparison to the engine, as well as when it makes sense to introduce the API offerings to the lender. Both the standalone PPE and use of the APIs require a pricing engine contract, the main difference being cost structure.
- Why API products are important today
- The processes and workflows in the mortgage lifecycle have changed, and more specifically, the expectation of the lender has changed (i.e., borrowers demand relevant purchase information at their fingertips – or they may go elsewhere)
- APIs facilitate a lender’s ability to meet borrower demand at that point of need –
from showcasing the latest mortgage rates to proactively delivering unique and tailored offers to their individual borrowers
- This strategy is central to Polly’s mission to modernize and transform mortgage processes so lenders can work faster, smarter, more efficiently, and better serve their borrower base
- Polly announced the expansion of our API offerings at a high level via this press release in August. The bullets below summarize our APIs in a way that is more applicable to everyday use cases a lender may experience.
- Product and Pricing Engine (PPE) API
- Also referred to as the Scenario Pricing API, this interface enables the user to submit a loan request and quickly yield results based on a variety of granular borrower- and property-based parameters.
- The PPE API still leverages the full functionality of Polly’s cloud-native, high-performance PPE. In addition, it can be positioned as a marketing and/or relationship building tool that enables the lender to establish and maintain better relationships with their borrowers. In the simplest terms: the API allows the lender to leverage the Polly PPE core capabilities while delivering a seamless experience within their branded solution.
- Benefit to the lender: By leveraging the API on their retail marketing sites (i.e., the lender website), they maintain full control over the borrower experience. The API plugs into their existing interface and brand, so as to deliver a cohesive and seamless experience for the borrower where they can see results in real time on the lender site, versus the need to go to another third-party website (like Zillow).
- Benefit to the borrower: Information available on demand, from their lender of choice.
- NOTE: We also offer an Initial Lock API that integrates seamlessly with the pricing engine.
This interface is a complement to Polly’s lock desk workflow automation suite and enables the lender to run unique scenarios and lock in loan prices all from one unified system.
- Questions on cost structure
- Q: If I only want to use the API, why do I need a pricing engine contract?
- In order to use the API, you still have to have implemented products, rules, etc. into Polly’s pricing engine. This does require a level of implementation and thus, incurs a cost. Further, an API-only customer will still leverage the full functionality of the pricing engine and the configuration UIs, but because they utilize an API and do not have users, they are only charged on a per-API call basis. A traditional customer is billed at a user-based tier.
- Q: Can I use the standalone PPE and the PPE API?
- While uncommon, we do have customer partners that use both the traditional user model and also leverage our APIs. In that case, the customer is required to sign a contract for the standard user model as well as a volume-based API contract.

## Archived DOCX: Lender Intelligence_ Data privacy, de-identification, and aggregation.docx

- LENDER INTELLIGENCE:
- Data privacy, de-identification, and aggregation
- —
- Protecting data is foundational to Polly's platform. Our Lender Intelligence features are designed to provide powerful analytics and AI-driven insights without ever compromising the privacy, security, or competitive positioning of our customer partners.
- Handling sensitive data
- Personally identifiable information (PII) is completely removed from all Lender Intelligence datasets.
- Non-PII data is de-identified, ensuring that individual organizations cannot be reverse-engineered or inferred from trends and/or metrics.
- Aggregates are designed to prevent attribution to specific institutions. Rather than isolating data from individual lenders, our models aggregate information across peer groups, institution types, and other binned categorical features like FICO and LTV. This cohort-based analysis ensures that all outputs reflect group-level behavior, not individual institution trends.
- All comparative dashboards and insights are curated specifically to highlight broader market trends without exposing or implying organization-level performance.
- Data deletion and de-identification
- De-identification is a multi-step, automated process that occurs during data ingestion into our analytics environment. The process includes:
- REMOVAL OF ALL PII: Any data fields that could directly or indirectly identify an individual, such as names, addresses, and/or phone numbers, are not included or are removed entirely.
- TRANSFORMATION OF NON-PII FIELDS: We apply binning to fields like LTV and FICO to ensure these fields cannot be linked back to individuals.
- FILTER AGGREGATION BY COHORT OR SEGMENT: Data is grouped into statistically significant cohorts (e.g., by institution type) which further obfuscates individual contributor information.
- Clients are only presented with aggregate insights, and no filters or tools are available in the UI that would allow disaggregation or drill down to an identifiable level.
- This process is fully automated, consistently applied, and is not subject to manual overrides by users or internal teams, which helps maintain Polly's strict adherence to data privacy standards.
- There is no audit trail or remnants in place to reverse engineer or re-identify the data.
- Appendix: Field-specific details
- The following fields/data are removed and never shared with the aggregation pipelines or analytics product.
- All PII-related data and fields
