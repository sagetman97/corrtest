# Documentation Quality Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist before treating a page as reliable reference for chat answers.

## Content Quality

- Page has one clear scope.
- Headings are descriptive and ordered.
- Terms are consistent with `docs/glossary.md`.

## Traceability

- Owner is listed.
- Last reviewed date is listed.
- Source(s) are listed when factual claims are made.

## Retrieval Quality

- Important facts are explicit and not buried in long paragraphs.
- Lists/tables are used for repeated structured information.
- Related pages are cross-linked.

## Safety and Privacy

- No credentials or secret values.
- No private HR or sensitive customer details in broad-reference pages.
- Unverified information is labeled clearly.
